Google Analytics Setup Instructions

For The Dutch Queen Unplugged Website

Good News! Google Analytics is already installed on your website. You just need to connect it to your Google account and get your tracking ID.

What is Google Analytics?

Google Analytics is a FREE tool from Google that tracks your website visitors and provides valuable insights:

  • Visitor Statistics: How many people visit your website
  • Traffic Sources: Where visitors come from (Google, Facebook, ads, direct links)
  • Geographic Data: Which countries and cities your visitors are from
  • Device Information: Desktop vs mobile vs tablet usage
  • Popular Sections: Which parts of your website people visit most
  • Ad Performance: Track how well your ads perform (especially your /#shows links)

Setup Instructions

1Create a Google Analytics Account

  1. Go to https://analytics.google.com
  2. Sign in with your Google account (or create one if you don't have one)
  3. Click "Start measuring" or "Admin" (bottom left)
  4. Click "Create Account"

2Configure Your Account

Account Setup:

  • Account Name: "The Dutch Queen" or "Band Management"
  • Check all data sharing settings (recommended)
  • Click "Next"

Property Setup:

  • Property Name: "The Dutch Queen Unplugged"
  • Reporting Time Zone: (GMT+01:00) Amsterdam
  • Currency: Euro (EUR)
  • Click "Next"

Business Information:

  • Industry: "Arts & Entertainment"
  • Business Size: Select appropriate size
  • How you plan to use Google Analytics: Check relevant options
  • Click "Create"

Accept the Terms of Service when prompted.

3Set Up Data Stream

  1. Select "Web" as the platform
  2. Enter your website details:
    • Website URL: https://thedutchqueenunplugged.com
    • Stream Name: "Unplugged Website"
  3. Click "Create Stream"

4Get Your Measurement ID

After creating the stream, you'll see your tracking details:

MEASUREMENT ID: This will look like G-XXXXXXXXXX

Important: Copy this ID - you'll need it in the next step!

You can always find this later in: Admin → Data Streams → [Your Stream]

5Add Measurement ID to Your Website

Send the Measurement ID to your web developer with these instructions:

For Developer: Update the file .env.local in the project root directory.

Replace G-XXXXXXXXXX with the actual Measurement ID: 
NEXT_PUBLIC_BAND_ID=the-dutch-queen-unplugged

# Google Analytics 4 - Get your measurement ID from https://analytics.google.com
# Format: G-XXXXXXXXXX (replace with your actual ID)
NEXT_PUBLIC_GA_MEASUREMENT_ID=G-XXXXXXXXXX

Example: If your ID is G-ABC123DEF4, it should look like:

NEXT_PUBLIC_GA_MEASUREMENT_ID=G-ABC123DEF4

6Deploy to Production

After adding the Measurement ID:

  1. Your developer needs to deploy the updated configuration to production
  2. Google Analytics will ONLY track on the live website (not during development)
  3. This prevents test traffic from polluting your real visitor data

7Verify It's Working

  1. Open your live website: https://thedutchqueenunplugged.com
  2. Open Google Analytics in another tab
  3. Go to Reports → Realtime
  4. You should see yourself as a visitor in real-time!
  5. Try visiting different sections (Shows, Gallery, About)
Test Your Ad Links:
Visit https://thedutchqueenunplugged.com/#shows to verify:
  • The page loads and scrolls to the Shows section
  • Google Analytics tracks the visit with the #shows hash in the URL

Important Notes

About Your Ads

Your ad links to thedutchqueenunplugged.com/#shows will work perfectly! The setup automatically:

  • Tracks visitors who click your ads
  • Records which section they landed on (#shows, #gallery, etc.)
  • Shows you which ads are driving the most traffic

Privacy & GDPR (Netherlands/EU)

Since you're operating in the Netherlands (EU), you may be legally required to:

  • Add a cookie consent banner to your website
  • Get visitor permission before tracking
  • Provide a privacy policy

Recommendation: Consult with a legal professional about EU privacy requirements.

Data Collection Timeline

  • Realtime Reports: Available immediately
  • Standard Reports: Data appears within 24-48 hours
  • Full Features: Some reports need 30 days of data to show trends

Troubleshooting

Not seeing any data?

  • Verify the Measurement ID is correct (check for typos)
  • Confirm the website has been deployed with the new configuration
  • Make sure you're looking at the Realtime report (not historical reports)
  • Check that you're visiting the live website (not localhost)
  • Try clearing your browser cache and visiting again

Need Help?

Contact your web developer if you:

  • Can't find the .env.local file
  • Don't know how to deploy the changes
  • See errors after adding the Measurement ID
  • Need help setting up more advanced tracking
